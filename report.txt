Trader Performance vs Market Sentiment Analysis

Objective:
To analyze the relationship between market sentiment and trader performance using historical trading data and the Fear & Greed Index.

Work Done:
- Loaded and cleaned both datasets
- Converted timestamps into a common date format
- Merged trader and sentiment datasets on date
- Calculated average PnL, total PnL, and win rate by sentiment
- Analyzed profit against Fear & Greed index values
- Compared BUY and SELL performance
- Analyzed direction-based win rates and PnL

Key Findings:
1. Fear sentiment produced the highest total profit.
2. Extreme Greed produced the highest average profit per trade.
3. Extreme Greed also had the highest win rate.
4. Neutral sentiment showed relatively lower profitability.
5. SELL trades had higher average profit than BUY trades.
6. Trader performance varied by both market sentiment and trade direction.

Conclusion:
The analysis shows that market sentiment significantly affects trader profitability. Strong emotional market conditions, especially Fear and Extreme Greed, created better trading opportunities.
